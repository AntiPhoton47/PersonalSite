I am a theoretical physics doctoral student working in the [group of Hans Briegel at the University of Innsbruck](https://www.uibk.ac.at/en/th-physik/qic-group/). My research experience is based mostly in general relativity, reinforcement learning, quantum chemistry, and scientific computing. My research interests currently reside primarily in the overlap between quantum foundations and information, artificial intelligence, and quantum gravity. I am particularly interested in developing quantum machine learning in relativistic settings and exploring the relationship between agency and spacetime structure. My most recent work has been in classical machine learning where my collaborators and I further developed an explainable model and introduced a quantum-inspired inductive bias to aid learning.

- Current position: Theoretical Physics Ph. D. Candidate
- Affiliations:
  - Working Member of COST Action [CA23115](https://rqi-cost.org/)
  - Member of the International Society for Relativistic Quantum Information [(ISRQI)](https://isrqi.net/)

## Values and Causes

Outside of research itself, I care about causes that reward clear thinking, broad compassion, and genuine usefulness. That includes strong scientific communication, education, better ways of doing good and helping those in need, and responsible approaches to artificial intelligence. I am especially interested in the effective altruism movement and related efforts to use evidence and careful reasoning to improve how we help others.

I wanted that part of the site to be explicit rather than buried in passing remarks, so I have collected it on a separate [Causes](/causes/) page.
